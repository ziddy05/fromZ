Long Ass Name

My full name is Francheska Patricia DG. Pati Cruz. Also known as Iska, Patchi, Pat, Pati, and Cheska. Quite a number of nicknames? Yeah. I was born on August 31, 2005, just exactly the date I told you. I love drawing, you know, just the usual thing I do. I also love singing though I'm not too good at it most of the time. I was an achiever, always called as the "brain" of every group of friends I've been in. I was admired and hated at the same time (but it never shook me at all, not even once.).

The Me

I like it when my egg is cooked sunny side up, a little saltier than the way you cook eggs (probably). My favorite dish is Kare-Kare. I love sweets, I have a sweet tooth. I like being alone. I love coffee! So much that I developed caffeine addiction HAHAHA. I like my feet peeking out of the blanket everytime I sleep, plus a pillow in my arms to accompany me in my dreams. I think of you a lot, it makes my head throb. I like flowers, my favorite are orchids. I like it when the moon looks like a toenail (crescent). I like my bathwater warm. I love spice, but I also hate it because it affects my hearing. I love anime, my favorite is My Hero Academia. I like it when you call me pogi, or compliment me, even for things as simple as telling me I'm good right after I eat my lunch and dinner. I like talking to you a lot, the thought alone it soothes me.


I think that's all(?) Ask me about anything, anything at all. I'll answer you each time, baby.

I Stay With You

by Zael

I cannot rearrange the world to your liking, nor press the stars into patterns that soothe your storms. But I'll walk with you through its storms and sharp edges, I'll carry the silence when words are too much, offer you a moment— a small space of quiet breath where the chaos holds still and the weight of things becomes lighter, and where the wildflowers grow back through the Earth's cracks. Let me hold your hand through it all, and I will stay with you through every fall.

The Love That Remembers

by Zael

You are the kind of love that remembers without being told. The love that pulls me back when I'm lost— the lighthouse that guides me home if ever I falter or drift far away, and the shelter I seek when the world won't conform. You are the kind of love that doesn't need to be asked. You know when to speak and when to just sit with me in silence. You show up. You stay. You see every version of me that I sometimes forget exists. And somehow, you know it all— what I was, what I am, what I need— without making me feel like a test to be passed, but like a book you want to learn and love— a story you're eager to read.

You are the kind of love that knows— the remembering that makes me whole.

Safe Haven

by Zael

There's a softness in your hands that I can't explain— how they smooth over the dents my past left on me, how they caress me in such a way that chases away the worry that's gnawing at me from within. You took my hand, pulled me close, and held me with gentleness and warmth— like a balm to my hurting soul, like a soft whisper that said: "Don't let go. You're safe here."

I've been held before, but I didn't know this is how it feels— to be held by hands as gentle as the ones I always tried to give— to be looked at and seen through eyes that reflect the same affection as mine. It feels familiar yet new all at once— like coming home to a place I've been, but never realized I needed.

With you, there was never any pressure to heal faster or speak louder. You made it safe to simply be— like a presence that says "Take your time. I see you, and it's okay." With you, I found comfort— the peace I didn't know I was missing. Not the one that comes from being alone, but the one that comes from being truly loved— held, seen, and known.

Unfinished Canvas

by Zael

I've never seen myself as something whole, thinking I was nothing more than broken pieces—a canvas ruined by mistakes. I used to sell myself short, crushed by the hollow weight of being "just" and "almost enough". Yet still, you linger. You trace the broken lines others call flaws with your painted fingertips, naming them beautiful. You see art in everything—like an artist who sees meaning in the overlooked, light seeping through a door left ajar, and even in a worn, secondhand heart like mine.

You carry the gift of seeing what is into what could be—like brushstrokes waiting to fill the void and empty spaces. And so, though weathered, cracked, and imperfect, you've shown me that even I could still be worth holding—an unfinished canvas, but no less a work of art.

The Quiet

by Zael

I do not want less of you, and I do not demand more. I just want you as you are—no filters, no masks, no trying too hard to fit into the corners of my affection. I want the you who forgets about things sometimes, who gets motherly when I'm being childish, who sometimes is uncertain and indecisive, but always warm in the way you reach for me.
From your presence, I seek the comfort I give just as much—a balance, a breathing rhythm that hums between two hearts that beats for one another. With you, love does not have to be larger than life; it lives quietly even in the infinitesimally small gestures, like scribbling your name in my palm, so that even if I close my hand, I'd carry your heart with me in the ink pressed into the lines of my skin. Or writing little love notes in the back of my notebook that I would never let you read—tiny reminders that love doesn't always have to shout to be heard.
And when time pulls at us, as it always will, I will still trace your name in my palm—along the lines of fate where it may have been washed off, and in empty spaces, hoping you feel it somehow—the whisper of a love that never asked to be more than it already was.

I Carry Your Heart

by Zael

Your heart is easy to hold—not weighted by the walls built from past aches. It fits gently in these palms of mine, as if it were made for them alone and for no one else's. I pray, in the most selfish way, that only I get to hold it like this, that it's only me you'll ever trust with it.

I'll unlearn how to let go. So if ever your love trembles, let it find its way back to me. For I'll be here—arms wide open, heart steady—ready to face forever's cruelty with you, to embrace and choose you all over again.
